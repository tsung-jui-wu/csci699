Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs) have become essential for processing graph-structured data, yet the role of dropout within these models is not well understood. This paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of dropout in GCNs, revealing that it primarily mitigates oversmoothing rather than reducing co-adaptation, as in traditional neural networks. We establish that dropout induces dimension-specific stochastic subgraphs, offering a unique form of structural regularization absent in standard architectures. Our analysis uncovers that dropout effects are degree-dependent, introducing an adaptive regularization mechanism that aligns with the topological significance of nodes. We derive novel generalization bounds that incorporate these graph-specific dropout dynamics. Additionally, we explore the interaction between dropout and batch normalization, identifying an enhanced regularization mechanism that boosts performance. Our findings are substantiated by comprehensive experiments on node-level and graph-level tasks using 14 datasets, where GCNs, augmented with dropout and batch normalization, consistently surpass state-of-the-art methods. This work not only elucidates the role of dropout in GCNs but also provides practical strategies for leveraging this insight to improve model robustness and accuracy.
