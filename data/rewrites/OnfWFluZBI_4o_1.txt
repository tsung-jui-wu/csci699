Self-supervised learning (SSL) has achieved remarkable advances across various tasks, largely due to its potential to learn identifiable representations that correspond to the true generative factors of data. Despite this progress, the precise mechanisms by which SSL captures these underlying dynamics remain underexplored. In this work, we propose a novel framework, Dynamics Contrastive Learning (DCL), which extends the capabilities of SSL to identify dynamical systems within latent spaces. Our method effectively uncovers linear, switching linear, and non-linear dynamics, even under complex non-linear observation models. We provide rigorous theoretical guarantees for our approach, demonstrating its validity in capturing accurate system dynamics. Empirical evaluations further corroborate our theoretical findings, showcasing significant improvements over existing methods in accurately modeling latent dynamics. By bridging the gap between SSL and system identification, our work not only enhances the interpretability of learned representations but also broadens the applicability of SSL to domains requiring dynamic modeling.
