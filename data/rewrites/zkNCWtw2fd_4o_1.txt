Effective information retrieval across multiple languages remains a critical challenge in natural language processing. While multilingual pre-trained language models have achieved significant progress, they often optimize performance for monolingual, cross-lingual, or multilingual tasks independently, potentially neglecting others. We introduce a novel hybrid batch training strategy aimed at concurrently enhancing zero-shot retrieval performance across monolingual, cross-lingual, and multilingual settings while addressing inherent language biases. Our method involves fine-tuning multilingual models using a balanced mix of monolingual and cross-lingual question-answer pairs, strategically sampled based on dataset size. Evaluated on the XQuAD-R, MLQA-R, and MIRACL benchmark datasets, our approach consistently delivers superior or equivalent zero-shot retrieval results across diverse languages and tasks compared to exclusive monolingual or cross-lingual training. Notably, hybrid batch training substantially mitigates language bias, facilitating the learning of language-agnostic representations. These findings underscore the potential of our method to advance robust zero-shot retrieval capabilities across a wide spectrum of languages.
